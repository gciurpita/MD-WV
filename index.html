B&O (blue) and Western Maryland (red) mainlines
The roughly 70x36 foot layout has two multi-track mainlines, each is ~450 ft (~7.5 scale miles), with reversing loops at each end. The layout is designed for continuous running during weekend and week long (county fair) open houses. These mainlines model the B&O and Western Maryland from Cumberland to Connellsville, includes the Cumberland yard, engine terminal and recently (2024) completed Cumberland freight house.

The layout uses:

  • Digitrax a DCS210 command station
  • 3 Digitrax DB210 boosters
  • 35 Digitrax DS64s stationary decoders
  • Digitrax UR93 Duplex Radio Transceiver
  • Digitrax DT602D throttles
  • DCC Specialties PSX and PSXX circuit breakers
  • DCC Specialties PSX-AR auto-reversers

DS64s are used both to control turnouts (see Sw-DS64) and routes (see Routes, still collecting data).

Each mainline is divided into 8 blocks, separate blocks in each direction. Almost all turnouts are controlled from from the DT602d controllers using DS-64 stationary decodes and Tortoise machines. Caboose ground throws are used in the Connellsville yard and over center springs at the Cumberland Freight House

A limited amount of signaling is implemented using ESP-32 processors and block detection from PSX/PSXX/PSX-ARs. The ESP-32s share block occupancy information via broadcast UDP WiFi. A wall mounted display shows block occupancy on both mainlines with white LEDs, as well as shorts using red LEDs.
