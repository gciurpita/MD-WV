B&O (blue) and Western Maryland (red) mainlines
The roughly 70x36 foot layout has two multi-track mainlines with reversing loops at each end designed for continuous running during weekend and weeklong open houses. These mainlines model the B&O and Western Maryland from Cumberland to Connelsville. The layout includes the Cumberland yard, engine terminal and recently completed Cumberland freight house and has interior and exterior aisles for visitors.

The layout uses a Digitrax control system composed of a DB210 command station and three separate ??? boosters. Each mainline is divided into 8 blocks with PSX/PSXX circuit breakers for each block, in each direction with PSX-ARs managing the reversing loops.

Almost all turnouts are controlled from from the DT602d controllers using DS-64 stationary decodes and Tortoise machines. Caboose ground throws are used in the Connelsville yard and over center springs at the Cumberland Freight House

A limited amount of signaling is implemented use ESP-32 processors and PSX/PSXX block detection. The ESP-32s share block occupancy information via broadcast UDP WiFi. A wall mounted display shows block occupancy on both mainlines with white LEDs, as well as shorts using red LEDs.
